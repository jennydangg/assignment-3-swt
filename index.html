the Sweetener World Tour

Ariana Grande began her third world tour in March 18, 2019 in Albany, New York. The Sweetener World Tour was an arena show based tour that began its first leg in North America, making its way around the world to Europe. The tour's concept consisted of Ariana Grande's two most recent albums she released in 2018 and 2019. Ariana named the tour after her album "Sweetener" which was released in 2018. The other album released in 2019 is known as "Thank U, Next". Along with these two albums as the main songs on her set list, she performs numerous pop hits from all of her other albums such as "Break Free" and "Love Me Harder". Not only did Ariana perform alone on tour, she had a few guests who would open her shows for her. Ariana brought Social House, Normani and Ella Mai as her opening acts for the Sweetener World Tour.

Throughout the tour, Ariana and her team recorded the live concerts which was later used for an album. This album is known as "K Bye for Now (swt live)" which is the live version of the songs she sings on the Sweetener World Tour. The tour wrapped up on December 22, 2019 in Inglewood, California. After playing 101 dates of the Sweetener World Tour, she collaborated with Netflix to bring fans a documentary of the shows she performed. The documentary, "Excuse Me, I Love You" was released on December 21, 2020.
